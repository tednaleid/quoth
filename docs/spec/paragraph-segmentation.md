# Paragraph Segmentation

How Quoth breaks transcript text into readable paragraphs.

## Problem

YouTube transcripts arrive as a flat list of timed words. Without paragraph
breaks, long videos produce a wall of text. Different video types have very
different pacing -- a documentary with frequent cuts has natural pauses; a
continuous monologue may have almost none.

## Approach: Top-N Gap Ranking

Rather than using a fixed gap threshold (e.g., "break after 2 seconds of
silence"), we let each video's own timing data decide where paragraphs go.

### Algorithm

1. **Target paragraph count.** Divide total word count by 70 to get a target
   number of breaks. A 7000-word transcript targets ~99 breaks (~100
   paragraphs).

2. **Collect candidates.** Every word boundary where the preceding word ends
   a sentence (`.` `!` `?`) is a candidate. Each candidate records the
   inter-word gap (time between the previous word's start and the next
   word's start).

3. **Rank by gap size.** Sort candidates by gap descending and pick the top
   N. These are the places where the speaker paused longest at a sentence
   boundary -- natural topic transitions.

4. **Oversized segment fallback.** Any resulting segment over 150 words gets
   split at the largest sentence-boundary gap in its middle 50% (avoiding
   tiny fragments at the edges).

### Why this works

- **Adaptive.** A fast-talking monologue with uniform timing still gets
  paragraphs because we pick the *relatively* longest pauses, even if
  they're short in absolute terms.
- **Stable for well-paced content.** A documentary with clear scene breaks
  already has large gaps at sentence ends. The algorithm picks those same
  breaks, producing the same result as a fixed threshold would.
- **Sentence-aligned.** Paragraphs always start at sentence boundaries, so
  the text reads naturally.

### What we tried and rejected

- **Fixed 2000ms gap threshold + sentence chunking (min 30 words).** Too many
  short paragraphs. Ignores timing in the second pass entirely.
- **Combined scoring (gap + sentence + length pressure).** Hard to tune --
  the sentence bonus made it trigger on small gap variations, producing even
  more fragmentation.

## YouTube caption quality

YouTube's ASR system already provides punctuation and casing in the json3
caption format. The extension preserves this as-is (no stripping, no
lowercasing). Human-authored captions (`kind != "asr"`) have even richer
formatting including speaker labels and sound descriptions.

The `isAutoGenerated` field on `CaptionTrack` distinguishes the two.
Currently both are displayed the same way; a future ML formatting step could
enhance auto-generated captions further but is not needed for most recent
videos.
